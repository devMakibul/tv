Audio / Subtitle Setup

Due to strict browser security algorithms, embedded Audio Tracks and Subtitles inside MKV/MP4 files can be difficult to extract locally. Use a free video converter website or app to convert your video's audio to MP3 or AAC if it plays silently.

1. Audio Tracks (Experimental Flag)

Browsers hide local Audio Tracks API by default. You MUST enable it manually:

  • Chrome/Edge: Go to chrome://flags/#enable-experimental-web-platform-features and switch to Enabled.
  • Firefox: Go to about:config and set media.track.enabled to true.

Then restart your browser and click Check.

2. Subtitles Workaround

Web browsers almost ALWAYS refuse to read embedded subtitles inside local MKV files. To use subtitles, just hit the Subtitles icon in the player and select Load External (.vtt/.srt) to attach your own subtitle file directly.
